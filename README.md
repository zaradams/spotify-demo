Steer the Queue — Spotify Feature Prototype

**Problem**
Gen Z Premium users increasingly treat Spotify as background noise — sessions pass without a single intentional interaction, weakening their emotional connection to the product and increasing churn risk as prices rise.

**User**
Gen Z Premium users with low engagement patterns. Gen Z grew up with social media scroll behavior, making passive consumption the default — which makes active re-engagement harder to sustain and more valuable to solve. Low engagement is defined as habitual, passive listening patterns, such as replaying the song songs or playlists with limited discovery.

**Solution**
Steer the Queue: lightweight, in-session controls triggered by stagnant listening behavior that nudge users from passive to active, without interrupting playback. Chosen because it intervenes at the exact moment of drift, requiring minimal effort from the user.
Why it works: The goal isn't a tap, it's a moment of conscious listening. An "I love this song" reaction that makes Spotify feel irreplaceable versus any other music platform. Even a single prompted interaction deepens perceived value and strengthens Spotify's recommendation data.

**Metrics**
North Star: Session Discovery Rate — % of songs in a session not played in the last 30 days
Supporting: Prompt Response Rate — % of triggered prompts that receive an interaction vs. dismissal
Guardrails: Session Drop-off Rate, Subscription Churn Rate
